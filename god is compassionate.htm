God is Compassionate

Have you ever been moved by tenderness and compassion? Maybe you have found your heart filled with empathy at

the loneliness of another, or burdened by the plight of people being decimated by hunger halfway around the world,

or moved to tears by the yowls of a staggering, dying animal. I still remember as a six year old feeling a profound

sense of sorrow for a blind and handicapped beggar as he sat hunched in his tattered clothes on the

bustling streets of Hong Kong, banging his tin cup, asking into the darkness for coins. Compassion is to

feel the weight another bears. It is to sit with another in their suffering. It is to so identify with another

that their heart is in yours. Compassion is from God. To empathize, to sympathize, to understand

someone and care as if it was Himself-- this is the very heart of God. He is torn by the struggles and

heartache we face from living in this broken world. God is not distant from us, in some far flung heaven,

aloof to our pain or plight. No. He is very near. He is deeply sensitive to our need. And He feels with us,

what we feel. This is why we sometimes call Him, “Immanuel,” which means, “God with us.”

In his time with Jesus, Matthew shares over and over how-- no matter if the person seeking out Jesus was rich or

poor, morally filthy or seemingly clean, of high status or a social outcast-- he saw Jesus listening to them, caring for

them, and being fully engaged with what was on their heart. “When Jesus landed and saw a large crowd, He had

compassion on them and healed their sick.” (Matthew 14:14, The Bible, NIV)

Jesus so identifies with us that He says, “Then the righteous will answer Him, ‘Lord, when did

we see you hungry and feed you, or thirsty and give you something to drink? When did we see

you a stranger and invite you in, or needing clothes and clothe you? When did we see you sick

or in prison and go to visit you?’ The King will reply, ‘I tell you the truth, whatever you did for

one of the least of these brothers of mine, you did for me.’” (Matthew 25:37-39, The Bible, NIV)

God is compassion. It’s why, in the helpless state He saw man-- slaughtering one another, enslaving one another, and

abusing one another; chained by corruption, arrogance, and selfishness; ground down by hardship, suffering, and the

lack of justice-- He came. 2,000 years ago, Jesus came to dwell with us, suffer with us, illuminate the truth to us,

and to make a way out of this whole mess for us, if we would trust Him and follow Him. Left to our own devices, we

had no hope. God saw that. And because God cared about us, because God is compassionate, He came to give us a

hope and a future, though it would come at the greatest cost to Himself.

Who is God?
