God is LOVE!

LOVE. It’s perhaps the greatest and most wonderful feeling each of us has ever felt. It has spurred men to daunting

voyages and formidable tasks. It has driven mothers to confront impossible odds to save a baby. It is what

binds us to one another in childhood, and what knits us together in old age. Love fuels the heart and makes

it glad to sacrifice on behalf of another. Each of us is hardwired to be loved. And we feel significance and

worth when we are.

While in America there is one word for love, the ancient Greeks, recognizing there are actually many

different kinds of love, matched each of these loves to its own word. There is the love and warmth we

experience as children from our parents. This is a love called “storge” in the Greek language. There is the

love that bonds friends together, and that is meant to flow between us and our fellow man. The Greeks

called this kind of love “phileo,” or “brotherly” love. The passionate, sexual love that exists in the marriage union

between a man and woman is called “eros.” All of these types of love help fill us and fulfill us in different ways. Yet,

to possess all these loves would still leave us far from complete, because the whole of love is so much more.

You see, there is another kind of love. It is a love that the Bible says is of the divine. It is a love that transcends

all these other loves combined, and why, without this one love, we will find ourselves always lacking, always

searching, always yearning. It’s the kind of love that flows in heaven, a love of such unconditional commitment

and care, that, when it hits our heart, it begins to liberate every part of us with true life and joy! The love

of heaven is the best stuff. And without it, we are incomplete. Without it, we will seek out person after

person, relationship after relationship, experience after experience, thing after thing, in a vain effort to fill

what only the most wonderful love of all can fill. The love of heaven is the glorious love of God. It is an

unconditional love called “agape” by the Greeks, and it is the love we need to be complete.

Agape is a heroic love that would risk life and loss for you and your good. But agape is also a patient love, strong as it

bears with you when you are most hurtful, obstinate, or blinded by pride. It is kind and tender, keeping you at the

moments of your greatest failing and shame, or when you have not performed well enough in the eyes of the world

for them to feel you merit worth or acceptance. Agape never stops hoping for you-- even when you

have wandered far off or are filled with crushing hopelessness-- it is always actively at work to get you

back on track. It’s a love that protects you, perseveres with you, and seeks for your best, even at the

deepest cost to itself. Unconditionality is the powerhouse of agape. And this kind of love never fails.

As we think about who God is, God so identifies with all agape is in its fullest form, that the apostle John says God IS 

agape. In other words, if we were somehow able to look at God, we would see the incredible picture of pure and

perfect agape-- holy love-- in living form. In fact, the Bible reveals that Jesus is agape personified! He is the very

image of our Father God, of agape, in living, breathing form. Colossians 2:9 says, “For in Christ all the fullness of the

Deity lives in bodily form,” and, Jesus Himself says that: “Anyone who has seen Me has seen the Father.” (John 14:9b) 

When we see the marginalized and untouchables no one else would go near beam with significance because Jesus

touched them; when we see the forgotten, blind, and poor spring to life because Jesus listened to them and healed

them; when we see Jesus going against the prevailing thought and attitude of the day, caring even for people who

weren’t part of His family, friends, tribe, or race to such an extent that He would weep over them and die to save

them... that is agape. God is the greatest love possible. He is the love that inspires our heart to soar and song: the

glorious love that fills and fulfills us. God is the love that frees us to truly love and live! God is agape.

Who is God?
