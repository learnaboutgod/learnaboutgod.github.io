Don’t Let Yourself be Robbed!
We all face times that threaten to crush the life out of us, don’t we? Maybe you’re in one of those times right now, where you’ve lost someone dear, or had something precious unjustly taken from you. Maybe you’ve just received devastating news from the doctor, or had your world smashed to pieces by betrayal or rape. In your pain, in your frustration, in your anger, you may have asked, “God, if You are good, how could You allow this? Why didn’t You stop it? Do You really care about me? Really, do You?” And the answer is: God does care. He cares with His whole heart. He made you. You are precious to Him. How could He not care? Jesu
How do you relate to God? If you think God is angry by nature, you may relate to God out of dread: you do good deeds or give to the poor to try to appease God so hopefully nothing bad happens to you. Maybe, instead, your relationship to God is based on duty or following rules because you think of God as a taskmaster to be satisfied. Or, if you think He’s unknowable, you may not interact with God at all. And this is tragic. Because it’s hard to enjoy God if you’re skittish around Him, isn’t it? If you think God may blast you at any moment, if you’re uncertain you’ve done enough to satisfy Him, or if you’re unsure He even exists, it’s going to be hard to connect with God and enjoy Him. The reality is, God is love. He says so. (1 John 4, The Bible) And He wants to enjoy a relationship with you that flows with the purest love! Don’t let yourself be robbed of the greatest joy you could possibly have: the joy of being in a vibrant love relationship with the God of all creation!
