God is Just

The Holocaust. Columbine. Charleston. Rwanda. Syria. The Killing Fields. The list could too easily go on and on. So

much slaughter. So many crippled. So many raped, uprooted, and terrorized. Too much that has been lost. In each of

these cases, for each of these lives and little ones, we not only feel the profound sorrow of their plight, but also the

need to hold those who committed these unspeakable acts to account. What we are seeking is justice. Justice flows

out of our compassion and sense of fairness... out of our desire to make right that which is wrong. Because we care

about those who have been traumatized and emotionally dislocated, because we care about those who have been

violated and marginalized, we care about justice. All of us can see how justice is good and right.

God cares passionately about justice because He, too, is compassionate and filled with a strong sense of fairness. In

fact, the reason we have compassion and a desire for fairness is because we have been made in God’s image. We say

God is just because He wants what is right to be done. The truth is, the only one who can judge rightly and fairly is

God, since He is the only one who is pure (who is unbeholden to bribery, and whose sight hasn’t been corrupted by

sin), the only one who is omniscient (who knows all, and cannot be fooled by a clever argument or lie), and the only

one who is truth (whose understanding of right and wrong is absolute, unlike man’s incomplete understanding or

shifting whims). God hates sin because it hurts and destroys. And, because each of us is of invaluable worth, He cares

deeply when we have been sinned against: when violence, evil, or wrong has been perpetrated against us.

The psalmist, knowing God and His heart so deeply, writes: “The LORD works righteousness and justice for all the

oppressed.” (Psalm 103:6, The Bible, NIV) “The LORD examines the righteous, but the wicked and those who love violence

His soul hates. On the wicked He will rain fiery coals and burning sulfur; a scorching wind will be their lot. For the

LORD is righteous, He loves justice, upright men will see His face.” (Psalm 11:5-7, The Bible, NIV)

If God didn’t love us, He wouldn’t care if others did something to hurt us, because we wouldn’t be valuable to Him. If

God wasn’t holy and pure, He wouldn’t care if others did bad things to harm us, because there wouldn’t be a right or

wrong way to treat people. But because God is holy, because He does love us, He cares about justice. And for all of us

who have been abused, maimed, or raped at the hands of another; for all of us who have ever been betrayed by

someone we really trusted; for all of us who have been scarred emotionally, relationally, spiritually, physically, or

mentally because we were rejected, abandoned, or discarded; for all of us who have been robbed, beaten, or taken

advantage of and were powerless to stop it; for all of us who have lost a loved one because of someone else’s selfish

desire; God promises that there will be a day when all of it will be brought to account. A day of reckoning is coming

where justice that is good and right-- justice that catches even those who seemed to get away with all kinds of evil

and who acted without regard to what was right, justice that we will all see is fair and appropriate-- will be served.  

Yet, if we think about it, if we are honest with ourselves, we are actually all guilty of sin. We have all done things

that are wrong and that have harmed others. We, too, should be held to account for our sins against God and others.

It’s only fair, isn’t it? God cares about those we have hurt, too.

And, I don’t know about you, but I know I’d be trembling as I stand before the One who is perfect, whose standard is

perfect, as He looks over my life on that day and proclaims justice that is fair for all those I’ve ever hurt and for all

the sins I’ve ever committed. In fact, God has already told us that “the wages of sin is death.” This makes sense.

Because God is so holy and pure, He cannot be in the presence of any sin. God isn’t trying to be unreasonable, it’s

just that His character is of the purest purity. Because of His absolute purity, if I am tainted by any sin, even one, I

cannot be in His presence. That means I cannot be in relationship with Him or experience any of His goodness: and

this is death. The wages, the consequence, of my sin is eternal death.

Does God want it to be this way? No. He loves you. He loves me. He wants us to be with Him. But because He is pure,

and we are stained with sin, we cannot be in relationship. Eternal death is the sentence we will be given for the sins

we have committed. And, on that day, as God dispenses justice that is fair and right, who will be weeping the most? I

believe it will be God. Who created us with such love and tenderness. Who cherished us to no end. Who wanted

nothing but the best for us. Who didn’t want to see us suffering, separated from Him forever.

Is all hope lost then? Are we destined for an eternity of suffering because we have already sinned? Is it too late for us?

No. Because God loves us, He made a way by which we could be saved from eternal death to eternal life. All of the

wrongs we have done against others, all of our sins, have to be paid for. It is only right and fair for us to pay for the

sins we have committed. Because of justice, there is a death sentence that has been placed upon us for the sins we

have committed. Yet, Father God, because He loves us so much, made another way. That way is His Son, Jesus. Jesus

loved us so much that He took our place, paying the death sentence we owed, so we could live. He died in our place,

taking our punishment, so we wouldn’t have to die eternally. He paid the price, so we could go free. When we accept

Jesus’s death in exchange for our own, when we accept His death as payment for our sins, God promises that we get

to live eternally... that we get to be restored in our relationship with Him.  The apostle Paul says:   

“For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord.” (Romans 6:23, The Bible, NIV) 

Because God is just and cares about justice being done, it means we will all suffer eternal death since we all have

sinned. But because God is love, it means we can live eternally because of what Jesus did for us, if we ask God to

forgive us for our sins and we accept Jesus into our hearts as Savior and Lord. To find out how to accept Jesus into

your heart so you can live eternally with God, watch the videos on the homepage!
