Knowing God is knowing forgiveness. They are one and the same. The more we understand the nature of forgiveness, the more we understand God. If someone asks, “What is God like?” look at Jesus. He prayed for those who tried to destroy Him. Forgiveness and love, touch, taste, feel, and smell the same. They have the same effects. Inside forgiveness there is this decision that says, I am willing to let you destroy me. I am willing to give myself up for you. Even though I am suffering because you hurt me, I accept you in the same way I did before, as though nothing had even occurred. God’s forgiveness says, I give you permission to hurt me again. John 3:17 says God did not send Jesus into the world to condemn it, but to save it. If we accept God’s forgiveness, we are promised to receive a new life. Being forgiven by God is good. A burden is lifted from us. Our guilty conscience is taken away. We don’t have the sense that we need to punish ourselves anymore. Our secret desire to sabotage our lives is gone. Freedom makes all things new and buries the past. This forgiveness was shown to us in the most personal way. In His wisdom, God’s forgiveness is shown to us in Jesus. Spiritually, our evil created a price to pay. It erected a wall between us and God. Something in that relationship was not right. We were too weak to knock that wall down and too proud to ask for help. So God, in His mercy, overcame what kept us apart. He pierced through our pride and removed the penalty of our sin through one sacrifice of love that took from Him everything He had. It didn’t cost Him money, it cost Him His Son, Jesus. With respect to salvation, Father God’s plan was not for us to become incrementally better people. We do not become new again by following more rules. This is why His response to our cancer is not to say, “Try harder and sacrifice more.” His one solution is Jesus. Jesus, the Son of God, did everything for us by dying to save our lives. A pride of lions stalks a herd of elephants. The elephants form a protective circle to protect the young. When it becomes clear that the herd cannot move and the wounded will die anyway, an adult elephant runs out to the lions in the dark night. As we in the center of that circle hear the cries of that elephant being devoured for us by the predators, that is Jesus. He carries our personal evil away from us out into the night. By dying, our evil dies with Him. For many of us, something that extreme is the only thing that would reach our hearts. Jesus is water to us, but we are living and dying to sin. We are in a tragedy that is overcome only by the shedding of blood. Because there is life in blood, it is the blood of God that makes new life. This is why it is easy to be certain about forgiveness. What God did was not halfway. His solution came out of love. There is nothing more He could have done. There was no other better way He could have shown His love for us and made it clear once for all time. Once would be enough. Jesus is God. His death for us shows God’s love.
