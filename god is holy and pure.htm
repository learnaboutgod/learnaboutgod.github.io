God is Holy and Pure

As you get to know God better, you’ll begin to realize that, similar to us, He has likes and dislikes. One of the things

He really loves is purity. When something is morally and spiritually pure, we call that holiness. God is Himself

spiritually pure and perfect, a being of absolute perfection in every way. There is no moral decay in Him, no wicked

way in Him, and because His very being is purity, His every word is of the truth. God will not lie, and He doesn’t

even want to lie. It is not even in God’s heart to want to deceive us. Lying destroys trust. It destroys relationships. If

I can’t trust you, it means my relationship with you will remain shallow because I will be guarding my heart so I

don’t get hurt. I will not feel free in my relationship with you. So the very nature of who God is in His holiness

invites us into the freest, safest, most life giving relationship we could possibly have. It’s what our hearts long for.

We can trust God, because His purity makes Him trustworthy.

God hates not only lying, but lust and greed, too. We can begin to understand why God gets so sad and angry, as we

see the seductive grip lust and greed has on our world, and the ways people are used, exploited, and manipulated in

the drive to possess gain or pleasure. Over and over we watch people ruled by their addictions, powerless to stop

themselves from destroying their lives, their loved ones, and, sometimes, whole societies, as they seek drugs, sex,

power, fame, or a good feeling. God sees the corruption; the slavery and sex trafficking; the physical, emotional,

and spiritual poverty; and He knows it could be so different. Our world is crying out for holiness and purity. Out of

holiness flows good and life, and I think it’s why, in part, God loves purity so much. 

Because God is pure and holy, there is no sin or evil in Him. God, to His very core, is good, fair, right, and just. The

book of Isaiah reveals the degree to which holiness is the essential essence of who God is. Angels, when caught up in

the awesome, overwhelming presence of God, cannot help but cry out what they are experiencing of God: “Holy,

holy, holy is the LORD Almighty; the whole earth is full of His glory” (Isaiah 6:3b, The Bible, NIV). The purity God loves,

and the purity God is, has the ability to change the course of our world.

Who is God?
