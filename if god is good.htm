We all face times that threaten to crush the life out of us, don’t we? Maybe you’re in one of those times right now, where you’ve lost someone dear, or had something precious unjustly taken from you. Maybe you’ve just received devastating news from the doctor, or had your world smashed to pieces by betrayal or rape. In your pain, in your frustration, in your anger, you may have asked, “God, if You are good, how could You allow this? Why didn’t You stop it? Do You really care about me? Really, do You?” And the answer is: God does care. He cares with His whole heart. He made you. You are precious to Him. How could He not care? Jesus, God Himself, shares how He feels in His heart: “Are not two sparrows sold for a penny? Yet not one of them will fall to the ground apart from the will of your Father. And even the very hairs of your head are all numbered. So don’t be afraid; you are worth more than many sparrows.” (Matthew 10:29-31, The Bible, NIV) But, since God does care about us, since He is good, why then do bad things happen to us? The next four paragraphs take us back to what happened when God created man and woman, because it is a key to understanding why bad things happen to us, even though God is good. The Good Life God Gave Us When God created Adam and Eve, He made an unbelievably wonderful place for them to live, the Garden of Eden. Everything about this paradise was breathtakingly beautiful: the colors, the sounds, the scents, all of it. Even their tastebuds danced with life as they ate fruit that was so mouthwateringly scrumptious. Best of all, there was relational harmony between them, and between them and God. God made this amazing home for them because He loved them with His whole heart. Now, in the middle of this garden, God had planted two very special trees: the Tree of Life and the Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil. And God let them know they were free to eat from any tree of the garden, but from the Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil they must not eat, because if they did, they would change, the world would change, and the nature of their relationship with Him would change-- they would surely die. All that had been created by God to only experience life, would instead experience death. God Gave Us the Ability to Choose So Our Love Could be Real But, why would God put this tree in the garden if eating its fruit was so deadly? One reason is because God wanted to give Adam and Eve a very precious gift: the ability to choose. Love is a choice. In one sense, love is only love if you can choose to give it. God didn’t want them to be with Him because they had no other choice. He wanted them to be with Him because they wanted to. A real love relationship is where love is willingly given, not forced or taken. So the Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil being in the garden was a way for them to choose if they wanted to be with God (by not eating this fruit), or choose to be apart from God (by eating this fruit). And, for them to have a real ability to choose, God would honor whatever decision they made. The ability to choose wasn’t a sham, where, the moment they decided to eat the fruit, God stopped them and said, “Ha! I know you wanted to eat this fruit, but you don’t really have a choice!” No, their ability to choose was genuine. God would respect their choice: even if they chose to reject Him and His ways, even if the risk of death entering our world was grave. The Jeopardy in Choice: The Potential that Death Becomes Our Reality Now, why did eating this fruit have to lead to death? Why couldn’t it have led to life? Was God being vindictive, or trying to unfairly steer their decision, by attaching an extreme consequence (death) to the choice of eating this fruit (rejecting Him)? No, not at all. When God told them the outcome of eating the fruit was death, He was simply stating a fact. You see, God is life, and the very source of life. (In Him is joy, kindness, and all that is good. God is the essence of love itself.) If Adam and Eve ate the fruit, they would be separated from all this because they would be separated from God: this is what death is. Where there had been only joy, now there would be grief and suffering. Where there had been only love, now there would be hate and loneliness. Where there had been only a deep knowing of one another, now there would be misunderstanding and mistrust. Death isn’t just physical; it’s relational, emotional, and spiritual, too. Death is separation from God, and all that is of good and life. God wasn’t being vindictive, He was just trying to tell them the ugly reality that would result if they ate this fruit. Adam and Eve Choose Sadly, after being tempted by the devil, Adam and Eve chose to sin by rejecting God and eating from the Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil. In that instant, death entered. Relational breakage between us and God, and us and each other, entered. They now “knew” the horrific reality of what separation from God was like. And things that were not supposed to be, became our reality. Cancer, murder, decay, and natural disaster-- things we were not meant to experience-- swept in to become the anguished normal of our lives, as did betrayal, abuse, hate, exploitation and so much else that is of death. At that moment, our world was perverted, as were our desires, thoughts, and motives. When God made Adam and Eve, He had endowed them with phenomenal intelligence and wisdom, but now they had gained extra “knowledge”-- knowledge, though, that wasn’t at all what they thought it’d be. They had “known” what good was, by experience; now they “knew,” by experience, what evil was, too. Did God want it to be this way? No. That’s why, in love, He tried to warn them. But, in the end, it was their choice. All That Once was Pristine and Good has been Corrupted by Death Why, then, do bad things happen to us? Is it because God isn’t good or doesn’t care about us? No. It’s because God gave Adam and Eve the ability to choose. And for them to have an honest ability to choose, God decided that, as difficult as it would be, He would respect their choice, even if their choice led to death. We now live the aftermath of Adam and Eve’s decision, just as children are impacted by the choices their parents make. As a result, natural disasters, disease, and famine have become our reality. Yet, God didn’t just give them the ability to choose. He gave us the ability to choose, as well; and, just like them, God respects our choice. You may have had your life torn to bits because someone chose to drink and drive. Someone may have chosen to build houses in an area prone to flooding, leading to a catastrophic loss of life when the rains came. Someone may have chosen to betray you, take advantage of you, or spread lies about you, leaving your life in shambles. These are terrible, terrible things, and we need to be clear-- for our sake, God’s sake, and the sake of our relationship with God-- God didn’t cause these bad things to happen to you or me; these are the result of people’s choices.* We live in a world that is broken in so many ways, not just from the choice of our first parents, but from the freewill choices of so many others, too. Sin is when we choose to turn from God and His ways-- when we choose to not love God and those around us-- and death is the result. We do not need to read the headlines to know it has not gone well for us. God looks at the terrible mess our world is in, at all that has been wrought by selfish act upon selfish act, sin upon sin, bad choice upon bad choice. He sees the millions plunged into chaos and dislocation from war, the countless crushed by greed and injustice, the weak abused over and again by those in power, and all those left in the turbulent wake of genocide and corruption, grasping at the tattered pieces of their lives and dreams. And He just weeps. It didn’t have to be this way. Yet, God weeps over us not only because of the immense agony and suffering we experience and have brought upon ourselves, but because of the fullness of death yet to come. You see, for a time, vestiges of God’s goodness are still with us-- we can still love, laugh, feel joy-- but one day, and for all eternity after, every aspect of God and His goodness will be gone. No love.  No happiness. No peace. Only inescapable loneliness, unrelenting hopelessness, gnawing emptiness, and an absence of all that is good. This is eternal death, and it is the consequence not only of Adam and Eve’s sin, but of our own, as well. Is all hope lost then? Though God cares about us, has He left us to our own devices? Is eternal suffering-- eternal death-- our inevitable destiny because of the choices we have made?                                                No, there is yet a way. There is Hope Because God is good, because He cares about us so much, He did something incredible so the worst possible thing that will happen to us does not have to be our destiny. At the greatest cost to Himself, God made a way so our destiny could be one of eternal life instead of eternal death. And that way is Jesus. When we accept what Jesus did by dying on the cross to pay for our sin stained lives, we experience a new beginning, a new life, a new peace and joy that can begin to transcend even the worst struggles we’ll face in this lifetime. Because of what Jesus did, we can have a renewed relationship with God! This new life won’t be fully experienced until we are in heaven, but even now we can begin to experience the satisfaction of our deepest longing-- to connect with the most lovely being of all, God Himself. Jesus died so you could choose whether you wanted to be in relationship with God. But, only you can make that choice. Click on the videos on the homepage to see how you can be restored in your relationship with God. *”But, what about justice? What about fairness? Does God just let bad people get away with murder and all manner of evil?” you may ask. This is a great question we cover elsewhere due to the length of this current Q + A. Suffice it to say for now, God is just. He hates injustice. God detests oppression, and a day of final, fair justice is coming. God promises it.
