God is Creative

God is incredibly imaginative... it’s part of who He is! Look at the world around you. What kinds of trees do you see?

Each has its own shape, size, and way it functions. Wouldn’t it be boring if there was only one type of tree on earth?

Look at the birds that soar high on the currents of the wind... the ones that flit to and fro... the ones that hop

or run along the ground. Some chirp. Some hoot. Some even sing pretty melodies. Little to big, the artistry of

God is seen in each one. And-- whether it’s the snail that scoots, or the whales that swim the ocean’s deep; the

rugged landscape draped in starry night, or its brilliant transformation by light and shadow as the sun courses

through the sky-- all of nature in its diverse forms and interactions speaks to the glory of God’s whimsy!

Yet, God’s vast creativity is seen not only in the diversity of all He made, but in all the senses He gave us with which

to take it in, too. What would life be like if the only sense we had was taste? So much would be going on all

around us, but most of it would be missed! But God didn’t create with just taste: He used a wide palette of

hearing, sight, smell, and touch, also, so we could take in more of the wonder of all His imagination crafted.

The sophistication and simplicity intrinsic to God’s artistry is evident even when we elucidate the laws of physics He

set in place to dictate the workings of our cosmos, and make discoveries of the world beyond our natural senses. In

His ingenuity, God made tiny blueprints in the form of intricate DNA strands that not only unwind at the right time to

direct the formation of proteins, cells, and organisms from amino acids, but also pass on what was to what will be

from one generation to the next. God was the first programmer ever, and the code He writes is simply exquisite.

We could go on and on about all the ways God’s expansive imagination is woven throughout creation to help you see

who God is in His creativity. But perhaps the best way for you to connect with God on a heart level is to realize that

the joy you feel as you are being creative-- penning a poem, making something with your hands,

blending flavors to cook a tasty meal, or even just doodling-- is, on some small level, of the joy

God feels as He creates. You can begin to understand more of God and what He feels in His heart

because you are creative, too! Have you ever wondered why we as people love being creative? It’s

because we are made in God’s image, and He LOVES being creative! In fact, each of us has been

created as an original by God Himself. Only you have the unique blend of personality, talent, and intellect that makes

you, you. You bear God’s image, and reflect His creativity, in your own special way!

Who is God?
