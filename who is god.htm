Have you ever thought to yourself, “What is God like?”

This is such a great question, but maybe us trying to understand God would be like an ant trying to understand us.

How do you explain color to an ant? Or, beyond moist and cold, how much can an ant fathom of the reality of all a

cloud is? Can it feel the joy you feel as you look at a breathtaking cloudscape? Relative to us, the ant has a limited

capacity to perceive, think, and understand: it just can’t grasp the realities and truths we are aware of. In the

same way, God is so awesome, and what He sees, thinks, and feels so far beyond us, that He shares with us this

truth: “As the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways and my thoughts than

your thoughts.” (Isaiah 55:9, NIV) God operates in realities that simply dwarf our faculties and perception.

In spite of our lack, what is astounding is that God wants us to know Him. Not just know about Him, but know 

Him. God is relational. And, as He made us in His image, we are relational, too. We are meant to be in deep, vibrant

relationships with those around us. We feel joy and happiness when we are. Yet, we will always find that profound

peace eludes us, lasting fulfillment is never ours, until we are caught up in the most glorious relationship we could

ever be a part of. And the great joy for us is that God’s beauty, personality, and love is of such infinite depth, that--

unlike that interesting person you get tired of after awhile-- our friendship with God only grows in wonder as we are

delighted and challenged each new day by the deepening revelation of who He is. What, then, are a few things we

could share to help you not only know about Him, but maybe even begin to know Him?

God is Beautiful

God is Creative

God is All Powerful 

God is Compassionate 

God is Holy and Pure

God is Just

God is Love

There are so many facets to all God is that we could have written more. Much more. But the cool thing is that God

Himself shares a bit about who He is-- His thoughts, His feelings, His very heart-- because He wants to connect with

you! We encourage you to go deeper with God by listening to what He reveals in the book He wrote by inspiration, the

Holy Bible (the Book of John is a great place to start connecting more with God).
